The 2020 U.S. Presidential Election: An Overview

The 2020 U.S. Presidential Election was a monumental event, marked by unprecedented challenges and a deeply polarized nation. As the world watched closely, the U.S. citizens made their voices heard, resulting in a change of leadership and setting the stage for the next four years.

Election Results

The 2020 election results unveiled a close race. The choropleth map and bar chart depict the distribution of Electoral College votes across states. With a decisive win in key battleground states, the Democratic nominee, Joseph R. Biden Jr., secured the presidency.

Candidate Approval Rating Trend

The approval ratings of both Biden and Trump experienced fluctuations leading up to the election. Events like Trump declaring a state of emergency for the COVID-19 pandemic negatively impacted his approval. In contrast, the tragic death of George Floyd shifted some public sentiment towards Biden. Trump's personal battle with COVID-19 further influenced public opinion. Explore the trends and see how external events shaped the nation's perception.

House of Representatives Post-Election

Post-election, the Democrats secured a slim majority in the House of Representatives with 222 seats to the Republicans' 213. This majority grants the Democrats significant power in shaping legislative agendas and advancing their policy priorities.

Senate Power Dynamics

The Senate saw a close division with 48 Democrats, 50 Republicans, and 2 Independents. However, the Democrats, in caucus with the Independents and with the Vice President's tie-breaking vote, effectively have a majority of 51 to 50. This dynamic is crucial for legislative decisions and confirms the Vice President's influential role in the Senate.

Biden's Approval Rating Since Office

Since taking office, President Biden's approval ratings have seen shifts. While he began with a favorable rating, subsequent events like the Afghanistan withdrawal, US-Mexico border crisis, and rising inflation have impacted public sentiment.
